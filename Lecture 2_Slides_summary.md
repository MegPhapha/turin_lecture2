# Summary: Development Cooperation - Types, Actors, Scenarios, Levels

**Source:** `Lecture 2_Slides.pdf`
**Author:** Egidio Dansero, University of Turin

## Overview
This lecture deck provides a structural classification of International Development Cooperation, breaking down the complex landscape of aid into specific categories, actors, and scales of intervention.

## Key Classifications

### 1. Geographical Destination
*   **North-South Cooperation:** Traditional donor-recipient model.
*   **South-South Cooperation:** Exchange of resources, technology, and knowledge between developing countries (e.g., Brazil, China).
*   **Triangular Cooperation:** Partnerships involving two or more developing countries supported by a developed country or multilateral organization.

### 2. Actors and Channels
*   **Governmental:**
    *   **Central:** National governments and their agencies.
    *   **Decentralized:** Local authorities (regions, municipalities).
*   **Non-Governmental:** NGOs, private foundations, corporate entities.
*   **Multilateral:** International Organizations (UN, World Bank).

### 3. Conditions (Conditionality)
*   Requirements attached to aid (e.g., structural reforms, governance standards, financial accountability).
*   **Critique:** Can be seen as paternalistic, imposing the donor's economic or political preferences on the recipient ("Modified Washington Consensus").

### 4. Intervention Scenarios
The continuum of aid contexts:
1.  **Emergency:** Conflict/Disaster relief (Humanitarian aid, food aid).
2.  **Transition:** Recovery, rehabilitation, restoration.
3.  **Development:** Long-term implementation of reforms, systemic improvement (Peace/Stability).

### 5. Intervention Levels & Scales
*   **Policy:** Set of rules/guidelines to solve collective problems.
*   **Plan:** Long-term roadmap (e.g., National Development Plan).
*   **Program:** Delivery tool defining timing, budget, and actors (e.g., Malaria control program).
*   **Project:** Specific set of tasks to achieve a concrete outcome (e.g., Nets distribution).
