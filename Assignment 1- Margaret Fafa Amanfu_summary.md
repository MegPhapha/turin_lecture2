# Summary: High Modernism and Techné in International Development

**Source:** `Assignment 1- Margaret Fafa Amanfu.pdf`
**Author:** Margaret Fafa Amanfu

## Core Theme
This essay explores the tension between "high modernist" logic—characterized by a faith in rational planning, scientific expertise, and metrics—and the messy, complex social realities of international development. The author argues that while technical tools are essential for accountability, they often compress reality, missing crucial elements like dignity, trust, and cultural meaning.

## Key Arguments
*   **The Dominance of Metrics:** Development projects often prioritize what can be measured (rations distributed, enrollment rates) over intangible outcomes.
*   **System Coherence vs. Social Coherence:** Good systems design (e.g., biometric identification) does not automatically lead to social coherence.
*   **Case Study (Ghana):** Digital public service platforms in Ghana promise efficiency but often exclude those with poor internet access or digital literacy. This creates a reliance on informal intermediaries (cyber cafés, agents), altering the system's intended function.
*   **Innovation Pitfalls:** "Solutionist" approaches like hackathons or blockchain pilots frequently fail because they overlook local trust networks and governance dynamics.

## Conclusion
The author advocates for **"technical humility."** Rather than rejecting technology, the sector should view technical systems as instruments embedded within social ecosystems, not as standalone solutions. The ultimate goal must remain human flourishing, not just mechanical efficiency.
